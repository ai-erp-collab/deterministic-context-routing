# Additional Use Grant — Plain-Language Explanation

This file explains, in plain language, the Additional Use Grant referenced
in `LICENSE`. If the two ever conflict, the exact wording in `LICENSE`
controls.

## Why A Software License Applies To A Methodology

`LICENSE` uses the Business Source License 1.1 (BSL), a license designed for
software. This repository is mostly written methodology and templates
rather than executable code, but BSL's shape — free use below a threshold,
paid use above it, automatic conversion to an open license after a fixed
date — fits how this project is meant to be used and eventually released,
so it is applied to the whole repository: `Methodology/`, `Derivatives/`,
and `Templates/`.

## What Is Free

You can use this methodology in production, at no cost, if both of the
following are true:

- Your organization's total headcount with access to the methodology
  itself, or to any workspace, documentation set, or internal tooling built
  using it, is at or below the team-size threshold stated in `LICENSE`'s
  Additional Use Grant.
- Your use is non-commercial, or your organization is an accredited
  educational institution using it for teaching or research.

This covers individual practitioners, small teams, students, and
researchers without any additional step. The exact headcount number lives
only in `LICENSE`, so it never has to be kept in sync in two places — check
`LICENSE` for the current figure.

## What "Production Use" Actually Means

`LICENSE` defines "Production Use" narrowly, on purpose. Understanding the
underlying method — module concept, context routing, confidence labels,
session state — is not something copyright can restrict, and we do not try
to. What copyright can and does protect is our actual written text and
templates.

**Not Production Use — free for everyone, commercial or not, with
attribution:**

- Teaching a course, writing an article, giving a talk, or publishing
  commentary that explains or references the methodology's ideas in your
  own words, even if you charge for it.
- Quoting or citing the Licensed Work, provided you attribute it to the
  Licensor and this repository.

This is deliberate: the more people who understand and cite this
methodology, the more useful it becomes to everyone, including us. We want
it to spread.

**Production Use — needs the free-tier conditions above or a commercial
license:**

- Using our actual files, templates, or text — as-is or modified — as the
  operational basis for real internal or client work.
- Copying substantial portions of our text or templates into a commercial
  product, service, course, or publication without attributing them to us.

In short: talking about the method is always welcome. Running your real
work on our actual artifacts, or reselling our actual words as your own, is
what requires a grant or a license.

## What Requires A Commercial License

If your organization's headcount exceeds the threshold in `LICENSE`'s
Additional Use Grant, or your use is commercial and falls outside the
conditions above, you need a commercial license. See
`COMMERCIAL-LICENSE.md` for terms, or contact the address in `LICENSE`.

## What Happens On The Change Date

On the Change Date stated in `LICENSE`, the Business Source License terms
on any version of the Licensed Work published before that date convert
automatically to the Change License also named in `LICENSE` (currently
MIT). After that date, that version is fully open source with no size or
commercial restriction. Versions published later keep their own four-year
clock, per the Terms in `LICENSE`.

## Questions

If your situation is not covered above, contact the address in `LICENSE`
before assuming either the free grant or a commercial license applies.
